Children Of Zeus

Athena

Athena, the daughter of Zeus and Metis, is the goddess of wisdom, war, and crafts. She was born fully armored from Zeus's forehead after he swallowed her mother, Metis. Athena is known for her strategic skill in warfare and her role as the protector of Athens.

Apollo

Apollo, the son of Zeus and Leto, is the god of the sun, music, prophecy, and healing. He is one of the most important gods in Greek mythology and is often depicted with his lyre and associated with the Oracle of Delphi.

Artemis

Artemis, twin sister of Apollo and daughter of Zeus and Leto, is the goddess of the hunt, the wilderness, and the moon. She is a protector of women and children and is often shown with her bow and arrow, accompanied by animals.

Hercules

Hercules, born of Zeus and the mortal Alcmene, is one of the most famous heroes in Greek mythology. Known for his incredible strength and completing the Twelve Labors, Hercules was later granted immortality and a place among the gods on Mount Olympus.

Hermes

Hermes, the son of Zeus and Maia, is the messenger of the gods and the god of travelers, thieves, and commerce. Known for his speed and cunning, Hermes is often depicted wearing winged sandals and carrying the caduceus.
