Who is Zeus?

Zeus is the god of the sky, lightning, and thunder in ancient Greek religion, and he is the king of the gods on Mount Olympus. He is a central figure in Greek mythology and appears in many stories related to the founding and function of the cosmos and the gods. Zeus was the youngest son of the Titans Cronus and Rhea, and he played a key role in overthrowing the Titans and establishing the reign of the Olympian gods.

Interesting Facts About Zeus

  1. Zeus overthrew his father, Cronus, to become the king of the gods.
  2. He wielded a powerful thunderbolt as his primary weapon.
  3. Zeus was known to take many forms to interact with mortals, including an eagle, a bull, and even a shower of gold.
  4. He was often seen as a protector of the city, law, and order in Greek culture.
